Phase 1 · 2025
Legal Foundation & NRC Engagement
Delaware C-Corp incorporation, USPTO trademark filing, and proactive engagement with the NRC — fusion reactors have no nuclear fuel licensing for D-T fuel. Operations launched from 890 Research Parkway, Lakeland, FL.
Phase 2 · 2025–2026
Build NOVA-0 — First Plasma Device
Construction of our compact Field-Reversed Configuration (FRC) plasma device at our Lakeland research campus. Target: plasma temperature exceeding 100 million degrees Celsius in controlled laboratory shots.
Phase 3 · 2026–2027
NOVA-1 — Net Energy Gain
Scale-up to NOVA-1 targeting Q > 1 — the historic milestone where fusion energy output exceeds energy input. First direct-conversion electricity demonstration. Series A funding close: $100M target.
Phase 4 · 2028–2030
NOVA-2 Pilot Power Plant
Construction of commercial pilot facility in Polk County, FL. Target: 50 MW net electrical output, enough to power 40,000 Florida homes. First grid connection and utility power purchase agreement executed.
Phase 5 · 2031–2035
Global Commercial Deployment
NOVA-3 modular units (200 MW) manufactured and deployed to utilities worldwide. 10 commercial reactors online globally by 2035. Florida's Lakeland campus becomes the beating heart of the fusion energy industry.
Reactor Technology

Built in Lakeland. Powering the Planet.

⚛️
FRC Plasma Core
Field-Reversed Configuration plasma geometry — compact, efficient, and scalable. Our proprietary coil design achieves higher plasma pressure than conventional tokamaks at a fraction of the size and cost.
TEMP: 100M°C · DENSITY: 10²¹ /M³ · CONFINEMENT: FRC
🧲
HTS Magnet Array
High-temperature superconducting (HTS) magnets generating 20+ Tesla fields. Our Lakeland engineering team developed a novel winding geometry enabling 40% higher field strength at lower operating cost.
FIELD: 20T · MATERIAL: REBCO · TEMP: 20K
⚡
Direct Energy Conversion
Proprietary charged-particle energy recovery system that captures fusion product energy directly as electricity — bypassing steam turbines for dramatically higher efficiency than any existing power technology.
EFFICIENCY: 70%+ · TYPE: DEC · PATENT PENDING
🔋
Tritium Breeding Blanket
Lithium-6 breeding blanket surrounds the plasma core, generating tritium fuel from the neutron flux — making our reactor fuel self-sustaining from abundant lithium and seawater deuterium.
FUEL: D-T · BREED RATIO: 1.05 · SOURCE: SEAWATER
🖥️
NOVA Control System
AI-driven plasma control system executing 10,000 feedback adjustments per second to maintain optimal confinement. Developed at our Lakeland software engineering lab using custom neural architectures.
LATENCY: 0.1MS · SENSORS: 2,400 · AI: CUSTOM LLM

Lakeland Test Campus
50,000 sq ft research and engineering facility at 890 Research Parkway, Lakeland, FL. Houses NOVA-0 prototype, high-bay assembly area, cryogenics lab, and power systems test bay.
